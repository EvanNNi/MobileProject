Problem statement

Resale apps work well for buyers, but listing items is still slow.

01

Users do not know what they own

Brand, model and condition are often unclear from memory, especially for electronics and accessories.

02

Pricing is uncertain

Many sellers either underprice valuable items or overprice listings that never sell.

03

Publishing takes too many steps

Writing titles, descriptions, tags and location details creates friction before an item reaches buyers.

Core experience

From camera to marketplace in one guided flow.

AI recognition

Upload one or multiple item photos. Luma detects possible product categories, brands, models and condition.

AI valuation

The backend combines image context, user corrections and UK market research to suggest a price range.

Nearby map discovery

Buyers can search for items and open a map view that shows nearby product photos and prices.

Trading workflow

Firebase powers authentication, listings, favourites, browsing history, chat and seller item management.

Technical integration

Mobile sensors, cloud data and external services work together.

01

Flutter iOS client

Cupertino-style mobile UI, camera flow, GPS location selection, map exploration and bilingual interface.

02

Firebase backend

Authentication, Firestore collections, Storage image uploads and Cloud Functions for server logic.

03

OpenAI analysis

Vision and language prompts generate product drafts, identify multiple items and estimate UK resale price.

04

Mapbox location layer

Listings can be published with GPS coordinates and explored through a nearby product map.

User journey

A seller can move from uncertainty to a publishable listing.

  1. Capture or upload item photos on a physical iPhone.
  2. Confirm AI recognition results and correct category, brand, model or condition.
  3. Use GPS or manual map selection to add the listing location.
  4. Generate a UK resale price estimate and choose the final asking price.
  5. Publish to Firestore, then manage the item from the profile page.

Future work

What would improve with more time?

Stronger trust and safety checks, verified payments, richer item authenticity workflows, improved price evidence display and a larger user testing cycle.
